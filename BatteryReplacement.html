Battery Replacement

Materials:

  • Screwdriver
  • Pry tool
  • New 43 WH battery
  • Parts tray

Time: 1-3 minutes


Before repairing any device, verify that the student whose Chromebook you are about to repair does not have insurance.


  1. Turn off the device, if you have already not done so.
  2. Turn the device over
  3. Remove the 7 Phillips-head screws on the bottom of the device
    1. Always keep your screws organized!
  4. Using a flathead screwdriver or pry tool to pry the bottom panel of the device off.
  5. Try to stay away from the I/O ports, as the metal cutouts are fragile and prone to breaking if subjected to too much force.  Pry near the bottom of the left and right sides or the bottom of the device, near the touchpad and palmrest.
    2. It is best to pry off the backplate in the areas highlighted red.
  6. Once the back is off, the battery is located at the bottom of the device:
    2. The battery is outlined in blue.
  7. Remove the two silver screws at the bottom of the battery, outlined in red.
    1. The standoffs that hold these screws may have broken off from the casing. Use pliers to remove the screws and discard the standoffs. They are not necessary to holding the battery in place.
  8. Remove the battery device, being careful with the battery connector, circled in green in the previous image. It will pull out, but it is better to be careful in case of accidental damage.
  9. Repeat the process in reverse to install the new battery. Be sure it is a 43 Wh model and not a 50 Wh, which go to Dell A35s and will not fit anyway.
  10. Plug the battery in before re-applying the screws. If your standoffs are broken or missing, disregard this step and simply proceed to the next one.
  11. Align the backplate and press firmly to re-engage the clips that hold it in place. Re-apply the 7 Phillips-head screws.
  12. Update the ticket, if applicable.
