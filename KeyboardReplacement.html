  1. Dell Chromebook 3120

Keyboard Replacement

Thanks to the way the 3120 is designed and constructed, the keyboard and touchpad assembly is the body of the device. Take apart a HP G4 or G5 and you will see the opposite applies - the keyboard and touchpad are seperate compenents that simply lift out of the body.

Before repairing any device, verify that the student whose Chromebook you are about to repair does not have insurance.

Materials:

  • Screwdriver
  • Pry tool
  • New keyboard
  • Magnetic screw stray

Time: 15-20 minutes


  1. Turn off the device, if you have already not done so.
  2. Turn the device over
  3. Remove the 7 Phillips-head screws on the bottom of the device
    1. Always keep your screws organized!
  4. Using a flathead screwdriver or pry tool to pry the bottom panel of the device off.
  5. Try to stay away from the I/O ports, as the metal cutouts are fragile and prone to breaking if subjected to too much force.  Pry near the bottom of the left and right sides or the bottom of the device, near the touchpad and palmrest.
    2. It is best to pry off the backplate in the areas highlighted red.
  6. Everything inside needs to come out.
    1. There is one small black screw holding the DC jack to the keyboard deck. Don't unplug it from the motherboard.
    2. The display connector on the motherboard is delicate. Remove the tape first, and then pull the connector away from the motherboard, towards the speakers. Use the frame that holds the black tape, making sure not to pull up with the connector.
    3. There are 2 silver screws holding down the battery. Remove it and unplug it from the motherboard. See "Battery Replacement" for more information.
    4. There are 8 silver screws holding down the motherboard
    5. There are 3 silver screws holding down the daughterboard. The motherboard and daughterboard can come out as one unit.
    6. There are two seperate ribbon cables that transfer data from the keyboard and touchpad to the motherboard. Lift up the latches and pull the cables out. The cables are highlighted in green.
    7. The WiFi card is connected to the antennas in the display assembly with two small cables. Remove and save the plastic securing them and carefuly disconnect them. There is one small black screw holding the WiFi card down.
    8. The speakers are connected to the motherboard underneath near the battery. The connector is highlighted in purple.
    9. Leave the speakers inside the device for the moment. Remove the motherboard, daughterboard, WiFi card, and DC jack as a single unit and transfer them to a new keyboard.
      1. Pull the speakers out of the old keyboard and transfer them. Refer to the above image or the Internal Map for correct placement.
      2. On your new keyboard, plug the motherboard and touchpad ribbons into the motherboard
      3. Transfer and screw in the battery with 2 silver screws.
      4. Screw in the daughterboard with 3 silver screws and the motherboard with 8 silver screws.
      5. Screw in the DC jack with a small black screw.
      6. Screw in the WiFi card with a small black screw.
    10. On the old keyboard, unscrew the 4 silver screws holding display hinges onto the old keyboard.
    11. Make sure nothing is connected between the display and keyboard. You should have already disconnected the WiFi antennas and display connector, but double check.
    12. Act as if you are going to open the device while it is still lying upside down. Open the keyboard so it is 90 degrees (or approx. 1.6 radians), then close it again.
    13. Pull the old keyboard away and set it aside. Take the now-seperated display assembly and place it display-side up on the table. Grab the new keyboard, with the motherboard other components inside, and align it keyboard-side down. The two pieces should fit snugly together.
    14. Push the hinges down onto the back of the keyboard. They should align and click into place.
    15. Route the display cable and WiFi antennas around the hinges. There are small metal tabs that act as cable guides.
    16. Reattatch the WiFi antennas to the WiFi card. A tool with a small, pointy end here is very helpful here. Use the color guides on the WiFi card to figure out which wire goes where. Use your fingers to allign one cable at a time and your small tool to click the little wires into place.
    17. Replace the backplate.
