Keyboard Replacement

Thanks to the way the A35 is designed and constructed, the keyboard and touchpad assembly is the body of the device. Take apart a HP G4 or G5 and you will see the opposite applies - the keyboard and touchpad are seperate compenents that simply lift out of the body.

Before repairing any device, verify that the student whose Chromebook you are about to repair does not have insurance.

Materials:

  • Screwdriver
  • Pry tool
  • New keyboard
  • Magnetic screw stray

Time: 15-20 minutes


  1. Turn off the device, if you have already not done so.
  2. Turn the device over
  3. Remove the 7 Phillips-head screws on the bottom of the device
    1. Always keep your screws organized!
  4. Using a flathead screwdriver or pry tool to pry the bottom panel of the device off.
  5. Try to stay away from the I/O ports, as the metal cutouts are fragile and prone to breaking if subjected to too much force.  Pry near the bottom of the left and right sides or the bottom of the device, near the touchpad and palmrest.
    2. It is best to pry off the backplate in the areas highlighted red. Ignore the part outlined in yellow, which is Dell's fault since I stole that image from their website.
  6. Everything inside needs to come out.
    1. Unplug the display connector at the top of the motherboard
    2. There are two silver and two black screws holding down the battery. Unplug it and set it aside.
    3. Unplug and remove the speakers.
    4. Unplug and remove the touchpad and keyboard ribbon cables.
    5. Remove the three silver screws holding down the daughterboard.
    6. Unplug the WiFi antennas from the WiFi card and remove the cables from their channels on the fan.
    7. Remove the single black screw holding the fan down.
    8. Unscrew, but do not remove, the four black screws holding the heatsink down.
    9. Remove the two silver screws holding the motherboard down.
    10. Detach the small board under the battery.
